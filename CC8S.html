The Koenigsegg CC8S

Six examples of the CC8S were produced in total, making it one of the rarest Koenigsegg models ever. The CC8S was the first production ever made by Koenigsegg. It was the culmination of 8 years of development work that started out with Christian wanting to build his own car. It can go up to a speed of 390 km/h (240 mph). It can accelerate at a speed of 100km/h (62 mph) in under3.5 seconds. It has 98 RON inlead fuel. Its standing quartile: 10 seconds, end speed of 217 km/h (135 mph), and its braking distance have 32 m (100 - 0 km/h). It was produced between 2002 and 2003 in 6 examples, making it one of the rarest Koenigsegg models ever. 2 of 6 cars were right-hand drive. There was pre-production premiered at Paris Auto Show in 2000 and this was where the first orders for the car were taken. It was built in a house but based on a modular Ford V8 engine architecture. It’s engine pieces are; type V8 cast aluminium, 4 valves per cylinder, double overhead camshafts. Cam cover of carbon fibre. Displacement is 4,700 cm3. Compression of 8.6:1. Weights at 210 kg. Lubrication system is a dry-sump with unique oil spray piston cooling. Oil cooler, with SAE 5 W/30 fully synthetic racing, Oil tank capacity is 12 litres (2.64 gallons). The induction system is Sequential, multipoint fuel injections. Supercharge with 1.2 bar boost pressure. Intercooler. A two-door, two-seater with removable hardtop stable under the front boot lid. Aerodynamics efficiency is Cd. 0.297. Frontal area 1.825 m2. The completely flat underside of chassis. It’s the steering of rack and pinion power-assisted steering of 2.7 turns lock-to-lock with a turning circle of 11 metres. The car priced at nearly $660,000 and featured a 6.0 litre V-12 that produced 660 horsepower and 485 pound-feet of torque. World record In 2002, the engine in the Koenigsegg CC8S was certified by Guinness World Records as being the most powerful engine in a series-produced car, in the world. The engine produced 488kW (655hp). The Koenigsegg CCR outdid the record set by the Koenigsegg CC8S in 2002. The CCR was equipped with an uprated engine, which added more than 150hp for a total output of 806hp. Guinness World Records verified this output and this record in 2004.
